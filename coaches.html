Since arriving at Yale in 2012 as the Shizuo Kakutani Lecturer in Mathematics, Dr. Rolf has been dedicated to finding creative, evidence-based ways for students to learn mathematics and to support other educators in this same endeavor. Dr. Rolf has transformed an introductory mathematics course, Calculus 115, by taking advantage of online tools and interactive technology to encourage concept engagement and development of problem solving skills. Building upon a platform developed by Coursera, he has crerated a “flipped classroom” approach, developing 42 videos and associated preparatory problems that students complete before coming to class. In addition to his teaching responsibilities, Dr. Rolf has maintained an active research program. Early in his career, he focused on numerical analysis; his current work is in the Scholarship of Teaching and Learning. Recognized as an authority in technology enhanced education, Dr. Rolf has been invited to lecture at numerous academic institutions and conferences. Most recently, he co-authored Explorations in Complex Analysis, a textbool with accompanying online tools for concept exploration.

After earning his Bachelor’s degree from Baylor University in Mathematics with a minor in Russian, Dr. Rolf obtained a Master of Divinity degree from Southwestern Seminary. He completed his Ph.D. at Duke University before being appointed as an Assistant Professor at the United States Military Academy at West Point in 1997. There, he received the Commander’s Award for Civilian Service for outstanding achievements in faculty development and research performance. In 2000, Dr. Rolf joined the faculty at the United States Air Force Academy, where he initiated and coordinated implementation of technology- based pedagogy into both introductory and advanced mathematics courses. Dr. Rolf and his wife Ann have two children, James and Julia. In addition to thinking about how to help undergraduates learn mathematics, he enjoys cycling, woodworking, and gardening.
